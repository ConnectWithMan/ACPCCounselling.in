ACPC Colleges List 2026

Top Engineering & Pharmacy Institutes

Discover the best Government, Grant-in-Aid (GIA), and Self-Financed (SFI) colleges participating in the ACPC Gujarat counselling process.

Overview of Participating Institutes

The Admission Committee for Professional Courses (ACPC) handles admissions for hundreds of engineering and pharmacy colleges across Gujarat. These institutions range from highly ranked state universities to premier private engineering colleges.

Choosing the right college depends on factors like merit rank, course preference, placement records, and the type of institution.

Types of Colleges under ACPC

Institutes participating in the ACPC counselling are broadly classified into three categories:

  • Government Engineering Colleges (GECs): Fully funded by the state government. They have very low tuition fees and excellent infrastructure. (e.g., LDCE, VGEC)
  • Grant-in-Aid (GIA) Colleges: Partially funded by the government. Fees are highly subsidized compared to private colleges. (e.g., MSU Baroda, BVM)
  • Self-Financed Institutes (SFIs): Private colleges where 50% of seats are typically filled through ACPC State Quota and 50% via Management/All India Quota. Fees are higher. (e.g., Nirma University, DA-IICT)

How to Choose the Best College?

When participating in the choice filling round, consider the following parameters:

  • Past Cutoffs: Check the closing ranks of previous years to understand your chances.
  • Placement Statistics: Review average and highest packages offered during campus recruitment.
  • Faculty & Infrastructure: Look for colleges with experienced professors and modern labs.
  • Fees & Affordability: Balance your preferred college with your financial capacity. Government and GIA colleges are highly economical.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Which is the top Government engineering college under ACPC?

L.D. College of Engineering (LDCE) in Ahmedabad is widely considered the top government engineering college in Gujarat due to its high placements, low fees, and legacy.

Are DA-IICT and Nirma University included in ACPC?

Yes. A specific percentage of seats (usually 50% for Nirma, and 50% for DA-IICT state quota) are filled through the centralized ACPC counselling process based on GUJCET/JEE Main merit.

How can I find the complete list of all participating colleges?

The complete, official list of participating colleges along with their approved seat matrix and fee structure is published on the official ACPC website (gujacpc.admissions.nic.in) before the choice filling process begins.
